Attributes & Skills

Classic Roll

Rolls dice and counts face value adding in any pips.

Successes Roll

Rolls dice and counts successes which are dice with a face value of 3 or greater.

Luck Roll

Performs a luck test by rolling the specified number of dice with each "6" counting as one luck point.

Unluck Roll

Performs an uluck test by rolling the specified number of dice with each "1" counting as one unluck point.

Die Code: D+
MAP:

Multi-Action Penalty (MAP)

The total penalty applied to your action rolls based on the number of standard actions you wish to take.

Equal to the number of standard actions you are taking -1 (n - 1).

Acrobatics

Acrobatics

Performing feats of gymnastics, extraordinary balance, and dance (and related performance arts), as well as break falls. Useful for running obstacle courses or doing water ballet.

Brawling

Competence in unarmed combat.

Contortion

Contortion

Escaping from otherwise secure physical bonds by twisting, writhing, and contorting the body.

Dodge

Dodge

Slipping out of danger’s way, whether avoiding an attack or a sprung booby trap.

Flying

Flying

Maneuvering under one’s own power (such as with wings) or in null gravity.

Marksmanship

Marksmanship

Shooting guns of any type. Covers everything from small slugthrowers to vehicle-mounted rockets. Also covers most internally generated blasts such as those from the Attack, Ranged power.

Missile Weapons

Missile Weapons

Firing unpowered ranged weapons.

Melee Combat

Melee Combat

Wielding hand-to-hand weapons.

Riding

Riding

Controlling and riding domesticated mounts.

Sleight of Hand

Sleight of Hand

Nimbleness with the fingers and misdirection, including picking pockets, palming items, and stage magic.

Sneak

Sneak

Moving silently and avoiding detection, whether through shadows or crowds.

Throwing

Throwing

Hitting a target accurately with a thrown item, including grenades, stones, and knives. Also used for catching thrown items. (Using or modifying grenades as explosives for special destructive effects requires the demolitions skill.)

Stamina

Stamina

Toughness and ability to stay engaged in physical activities.

Fortitude

Fortitude

Physical endurance and resistance to pain, disease, and poison.

Intellect

Intellect

Reasoning, knowledge, and logical thought capacity.

Business

Business

Comprehension of business practices and the monetary value of goods and opportunities, including the ability to determine how to make money with another skill the character has. Business can complement charm, con, and persuasion when haggling over prices for goods and services being bought or sold.

Investigation

Investigation

Gathering information, researching topics, analyzing data, and piecing together clues.

Languages

Languages

Familiarity with and ability to use various forms of communication, including written, spoken, and nonverbal. Characters may choose one “native” language in which they have written and spoken fluency. Additional languages in which a character has proficiency can be represented by specializations of this skill.

Perception

Perception

The skill of noticing details and generally being aware of ones surroundings.

Scholar

Scholar

This skill represents knowledge and/or education in areas not covered under any other skill (such as chemistry, mathematics, archeology, interior design, etc.). This may be restricted to a specific field (represented by specializations) or a general knowledge of a wide range of subjects. It is used to remember details, rumors, tales, legends, theories, important people, and the like, as appropriate for the subject in question. However, the broader the category, the fewer the details that can be recalled. It covers what the character himself can recall. Having another skill as a specialization of the scholar skill means that the character knows the theories and history behind the skill but can’t actually use it. It can be useful with investigation to narrow a search for information.

Survival

Survival

Surviving in wilderness environments.

Tracking

Tracking

Following the trail of another person, animal, or creature, or keeping after a moving target without being noticed.

Spirit

Spirit

Strength of will and wisdom.

Animal Handling

Animal Handling

Controlling animals and making them perform tricks and follow commands.

Artist

Artist

Making works of art, like paintings, photographs, and music or literary compositions.

Power

Power

The ability to control your own power and perform unusual feats with said power.

Willpower

Willpower

The ability to resist mind control and other influencing effects such as being conned or otherwise persuaded into doing something a character wouldn’t normally do.

Charm

Charm

Ability to engage and influence others with words.

Command

Command

Effectively ordering and coordinating others in team situations.

Con

Con

Bluffing, lying, tricking, or deceiving others, as well as verbal evasiveness, misdirection, and blustering. Also useful in putting on acting performances.

Disguise

Disguise

Altering features or clothing to be unrecognizable or to look like someone else. Also useful in acting performances.

Gambling

Gambling

Playing and cheating at games of strategy and luck.

Intimidation

Intimidation

Using physical presence, verbal threats, taunts, torture, or fear to influence others or get information out of them.

Persuasion

Persuasion

Influencing others or getting information out of them through bribery, honest discussion, debate, diplomacy, or speeches. Also useful in negotiations, business transactions, and putting on performances (such as singing, acting, or storytelling).

Seduction

Seduction

The ability to deliberately entice a person, to engage in a relationship, to lead astray, as from duty, rectitude, or the like.

Streetwise

Streetwise

Finding information, goods, and contacts in an urban environment, particularly through criminal organizations, black markets, and other illicit operations. Also useful for determining possible motives and methods of criminals.

Technology

Technology

Your proficiency with the technology of the time.

Demolitions

Demolitions

Using corrosives and explosives to achieve particular destructive effects.

Forgery

Forgery

Creating and noticing false or altered documentation in various media (paper, electronic, plastic card, etc.), including counterfeiting, though tasks may require other skills to help detect or make the forgery.

Lockpicking

Lockpicking

Opening a mechanical (not electronic) lock or safe without possessing the key or combination, as well as disarming small mechanical traps.

Medicine

Medicine

Using first aid techniques to treat injuries, as well as an understanding and application of medical procedures, including diagnosing illnesses and performing surgery.

Navigation

Navigation

Determining the correct course using external reference points, such as stars, maps, or landmarks, as well as creating maps.

Piloting

Piloting

Operating any kind of vehicle or powered armor traveling on or through the ground, a liquid medium, the air, or space.

Inventor

Inventor

Creating, fixing, or modifying gadgets, weapons, armor, and vehicles.

Security

Security

Installing, altering, and bypassing electronic security and surveillance systems.

Systems

Systems

Using and designing (not making) complex mechanical or electronic equipment, such as programming and operating computers and manipulating communication devices.

A component of how much physical damage you may endure. Subtracted from all incoming physical and energy damage.

A pool which represents your health from which incoming damage is subtracted from (after Soak).

May be spent on rolls to add an additional Wild Die to your pool. Also used to improve your characters capabilities.

Character points are gained at the end of an adventure

Spent before or after a die roll is made to double the amount rolled on the dice

Typically replenishes at the end of an adventure but may be awarded in game.
